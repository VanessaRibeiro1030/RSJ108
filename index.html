How to Write a Satircal Column

Assembly time: However Long You Need for Your Own Personal Level of Satisfaction
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Whether you're a Journalism Student in training or a Stand Up Comic, it never hurts to know how to write funny. Everyone loves to laugh so read the steps below to hone in on the humor of writing!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Patience
  • Creativity
  • A writing medium (i.e laptop, journal,etc)
  • Respect for different identities

  1. Discover What You're Going To Write About

    Choose topics that not only peak your own personal interest, but have relevance and will appeal to the general public or whichever audience you're targeting.

  2. Choose a Satirical Style and Technique

    Will you be utilizing exaggeration? Parody? Incongruity? Reversal? Irony? The different approaches you can go for your writing are endless! Research and explore differnt styles of writing and satirical pieces; choose techniques that you personally find funny or moving or entertaining. Create content that you yourself would want to read.

    Try some of these techniques!

  3. Know Your Audience

    Now that you have an idea about what you want to write about and how you want to convey that message, think about what type of audience will take appeal to your work. Whether it be the dating of the refrences you're making or niche sub-genre you're delving into- you'll always have one group of people more inclined to to read your work than others. That being said, think of ways you can connect to them even more. Satire is supposed to take a side, so choose which side you're going for.

  4. Be Respectful of Different Cultures and Identities

    Be careful to stay away from libel and slander, and keep in mind how different audiences will percieve your work. It's okay if not everyone finds it funny or understands the humor, but steer clear from being downright offensive or crude in any way.

    Keep ethnicity, race, sexual and gender orientation, and other identities in mind!

  5. Start Writing!

    Begin with goals in mind and always be thinking of what you're trying to get across. Specifically in satire, exaggerations, irony, and humor all work exceeingly well- just try to think of doing it in a way that is creative and entertaining. Good luck; you've got this!
